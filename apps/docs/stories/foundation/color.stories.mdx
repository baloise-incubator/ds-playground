## Color Overview

Colors play a central role in the Baloise Design System, serving as a means to visually convey information,
functionality, and personality in our experiences.
We are dedicated to adhering to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines AA standard for contrast ratios.

### Core Color

Blue dictates the visual appearance of Baloise and is present on every site. The Logo, text, buttons and links are blue.

<ColorCards>
  <ColorCard background color="alias.primary" />
</ColorCards>

### Typography Color

The main color for text is the core blue color and for dark backgrounds the inverted core color white.

<ColorCards>
  <ColorCard background color="alias.primary" />
  <ColorCard background color="base.white" />
</ColorCards>

### Backgrounds

There are eight colors which can be used for backgrounds.
However, the white color represents the default. The other colors should be used only if the context allows.

### Primary colors

The primary colors play the main role in the brand identity. A vibrant set of fresh shades is the center point of the color palette. They are used to provide accessibility, simplicity, and consistency throughout all brand communications. They are used for Baloise Shapes, Illustrations, background colors and to highlight design elements.

<ColorCardRow color="base.green" />
<ColorCardRow color="base.purple" />
<ColorCardRow color="base.red" />
<ColorCardRow color="base.yellow" />

### Neutral colors

Neutral colors are typically employed for text, borders, and subtle backgrounds when one does not want to draw too much attention to a specific design element.

<ColorCardRow color="base.grey" />

### Functional Colors

These colors are a functional extension of the accent color and are meant to be used only for specific cases and situations.

<ColorCardRow color="base.primary" />
<ColorCardRow color="base.light-blue" />

### Alert colors

To draw attention to important information or actions, the Alert colors palette is utilized.

<ColorCardRow color="base.info" />
<ColorCardRow color="base.success" />
<ColorCardRow color="base.warning" />
<ColorCardRow color="base.danger" />

## Color Combinations

Color combinations always need to stay in the same color spectrum. For example Purple with Purple Light. There is no color coding within the Baloise appearance. All Baloise primary colors are equal and should be
used as such.
